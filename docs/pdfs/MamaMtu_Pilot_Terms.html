2 • What the Pilot Clinic Agrees To

Week 1 — Setup

  • Designate 1–2 MamaMtu Champions (staff who will use the system daily)
  • Provide WiFi access, or confirm offline-sync configuration with the MamaMtu team
  • Complete the 2-hour training session within the first week
  • Post the privacy notice in the patient waiting area

Months 1–3 — Ongoing

  • Use the system with real patients who have signed the patient consent form
  • Enter a minimum of 10 pregnancy records per week during active clinic days
  • Send brief weekly feedback to the MamaMtu team (a WhatsApp voice note is acceptable)
  • Permit impact measurement: MamaMtu will track appointments kept vs. missed, records entered, and staff time saved — no patient-identifiable data leaves the clinic without consent

Compliance

  • Obtain signed patient consent before entering any patient data
  • Keep signed paper consent forms on file at the clinic
  • Ensure only designated staff hold login credentials — do not share passwords
  • Report any suspected security incident to MamaMtu within 24 hours

4 • Data Ownership and Privacy

  • Patient data belongs to the patient. The clinic is the data controller. MamaMtu is the data processor only.
  • Patient data is encrypted at rest and in transit, hosted on EU-based servers (GDPR-compliant), and accessible only to authorised clinic staff.
  • MamaMtu will never sell patient data or share it with third parties without explicit written consent.
  • Anonymised, aggregated statistics (e.g. total ANC visits across clinics) may be used for grant reporting and impact measurement.
  • Full details: MamaMtu Privacy Notice at mamamtu.co.tz/privacy

5 • Limitations of the Platform

MamaMtu is a record-keeping, workflow, and reporting tool. It is NOT: a diagnostic system • a replacement for clinical judgment • an emergency response system • a telemedicine platform.
All clinical decisions remain the sole responsibility of qualified healthcare professionals at the clinic.
