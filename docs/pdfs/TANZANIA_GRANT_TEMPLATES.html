2. PROJECT SUMMARY (250 words)
   
   MamaMtu is a digital health platform designed to reduce preventable maternal 
   and newborn mortality in Tanzania by improving health record management, 
   appointment tracking, and care coordination at clinics and hospitals.
   
   The problem: Many maternal deaths occur due to missed appointments, poor 
   care continuity, and lack of visibility into high-risk pregnancies. Paper 
   records are often lost or incomplete.
   
   The solution: MamaMtu provides an affordable, easy-to-use digital system for 
   clinics to track pregnancies, antenatal visits (ANC), delivery outcomes, 
   and newborn health - with automated reminders and reporting.
   
   Innovation: Unlike existing hospital information systems designed for large 
   facilities, MamaMtu is built for small-to-medium clinics with limited IT 
   staff and irregular internet connectivity.
   
   Impact goal: Reduce missed ANC appointments by 40%, improve early detection 
   of pregnancy complications, and support Tanzania's maternal health targets.

ORGANIZATION: Necuva Group Limited
PROJECT: MamaMtu - Maternal Health Platform for Tanzania

EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

MamaMtu addresses maternal mortality in Tanzania through a digital platform 
that improves antenatal care tracking, appointment adherence, and care 
coordination. The innovation is designed specifically for low-resource 
settings with unreliable connectivity.

INNOVATION (150 words)

Most hospital information systems in Africa are designed for large urban 
hospitals with full-time IT staff and reliable internet. MamaMtu takes a 
different approach: it is built for small-to-medium clinics that serve 
the majority of pregnant women in Tanzania.

Key innovations:
1. Offline-first architecture - works without internet, syncs when available
2. SMS-based reminders - no smartphone required for patients
3. Swahili-first interface - designed for local users, not translated from English
4. Low-cost SaaS model - affordable for private clinics, free for public pilots

HEALTH IMPACT (150 words)

Target population: Pregnant women attending clinics in urban and peri-urban 
Tanzania, particularly those at risk of missing appointments or experiencing 
complications.

Expected outcomes:
- 40% reduction in missed ANC appointments
- Earlier identification of pregnancy complications
- Improved care continuity between visits
- Better data for clinic management and Ministry reporting

SCALABILITY (150 words)

Phase 1 (Year 1): 10 clinics, 2,000 pregnancies, Dar es Salaam region
Phase 2 (Year 2): 50 clinics, 10,000 pregnancies, expansion to Dodoma, Mwanza
Phase 3 (Year 3): 200 clinics across Tanzania, integration with DHIS2

The platform is built on modern web standards, allowing rapid deployment 
to new regions. Revenue from private clinics will subsidize public 
facility deployment.

Tips for Tanzania Grant Success

  1. Lead with impact, not technology

- "Reduces maternal deaths" beats "uses React and PostgreSQL"

  1. Show local team presence

- Funders want to know you're not a remote foreign team

  1. Have pilot partners ready

- Even a letter of intent from one clinic strengthens applications

  1. Demonstrate sustainability

- Don't ask for perpetual funding; show revenue model

  1. Align with government priorities

- Reference Tanzania Digital Health Strategy, SDG 3, MoH targets

  1. Use local data

- Cite Tanzania DHS, WHO country profiles, not just global statistics

  1. Include Swahili

- Even a summary section in Swahili shows commitment to local users
